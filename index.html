Where to stay

Six neighborhoods, each with its own character

Barcelona isn't one city — it's many. We match you to the right one based on how you like to travel.

Gothic Quarter

Medieval heart

The oldest part of Barcelona. Roman walls underfoot, Gothic arches overhead, and plazas that appear when you turn a corner. Best for travelers who want to feel the age of the city — and don't mind getting a little lost.

Stays in Gothic Quarter →

Eixample

Modernist grid

Barcelona's architectural crown jewel. Walk the grid of Gaudí masterpieces, eat on leafy boulevards, and understand why this grid became the model for modern city planning.

Stays in Eixample →

Barceloneta

Beach & seafood

Where the city meets the Mediterranean. Fresh fish at the port, an afternoon on the sand, and a sunset that makes you forget you're in a major European capital.

Stays in Barceloneta →

Gràcia

Village vibes

A village inside a city. Morning coffee in Plaça del Sol, afternoon browsing independent shops, evening vermouth at the bar your host recommended. Gràcia moves at its own pace.

Stays in Gràcia →

El Born

Creative quarter

Barcelona's creative heartbeat. Art galleries behind old wooden doors, cocktail bars you'd walk past if you didn't know, and the magnificent Santa Maria del Mar anchoring it all.

Stays in El Born →

Poblenou

Tech & creative

Barcelona's reinvention district. Old industrial buildings turned into studios and restaurants — with the beach a block away. If you like cities that are still becoming something.

Stays in Poblenou →
